Publications

PortusSophia™ publications document the evolution of an independent research program investigating the architecture of disciplined inquiry.

The publication record spans peer-facing working papers, long-form books, archival declarations, and scholarly research profiles. Together they preserve both the development of the work and its current state.

---

Publication Philosophy

PortusSophia distinguishes between different kinds of publications.

Not every publication serves the same purpose.

Some preserve exploratory investigations.

Some introduce developing concepts.

Some formally declare stable architectural commitments.

Together they document the intellectual provenance of the research program.

---

Formal Declaration

The Declared Interface (PS-BIOS-001)

Status: Declared

The Declared Interface formally defines the scope, posture, boundaries, and methodological commitments of the PortusSophia™ field architecture.

Unlike exploratory research, this publication serves as the stable public declaration upon which subsequent work is built.

[View on Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/21192767)

Books

A Field Guide to Epistemic Failure

Status: Published

The Field Guide examines recurring patterns of epistemic failure observed across disciplines and institutions.

Rather than offering another philosophical system, it identifies structural failures in inquiry that ultimately motivated the development of the PortusSophia™ field architecture.

[Available on Leanpub](https://leanpub.com/afieldguidetoepistemicfailure)

Publication Status

PortusSophia distinguishes between publication maturity and research maturity.

Publication Status| Meaning
Declared| Stable architectural declaration maintained through archival publication.
Published| Publicly released work intended for general readership or scholarly discussion.
Working Paper| Research shared for examination and intellectual development.
Historical| Earlier publications preserved as part of the documented provenance of the research program.

Maintaining these distinctions allows the historical development of the work to remain visible while clearly identifying its current architectural foundation.
