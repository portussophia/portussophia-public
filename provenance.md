# Provenance

## The Beginning of the Inquiry

Every research program has an origin.

PortusSophia™ did not begin with an ambition to establish a new field architecture.

It began with a question that refused to let go.

---

Despite being an honorable United States Army veteran, an enterprise software engineer, and a contributor to healthcare interoperability and standards, I found myself unable to secure even a single interview after submitting dozens of applications.

It would have been easy to conclude that I was broken.

It would have been equally easy to conclude that the system was.

I accepted neither conclusion.

Instead, I became curious.

Artificial intelligence was rapidly reshaping the world, and I wanted to understand how these systems appeared to learn, adapt, and make decisions.

That curiosity led me into machine learning.

I encountered the stochastic approximation to gradient descent.

I did not yet understand the mathematics, so I continued asking simpler questions.

One question led naturally to another.

Eventually I found myself staring at something surprisingly small—an absolute value within an equation that appeared to be doing an extraordinary amount of explanatory work.

Why?

That question led me to postulates and axioms.

I learned that a postulate or axiom functions as an accepted starting point for further reasoning.

I continued asking.

Eventually I encountered a deeper observation.

A postulate or axiom may influence an entire body of knowledge while possessing no attributable identity of its own.

It may be accepted.

It may be foundational.

Yet its own origin may remain beyond attribution.

That realization altered the direction of the inquiry.

The question was no longer about artificial intelligence.

It had become a question about inquiry itself.

> **How do foundational structures acquire explanatory authority while remaining without attributable identity?**

---

## From Artificial Intelligence to Inquiry

The questions that followed gradually expanded beyond machine learning.

They became questions about distinction.

About boundaries.

About translation.

About representation.

About what is preserved as ideas move across disciplines, methodologies, and explanatory frameworks.

The work no longer sought to explain artificial intelligence.

It sought to understand the architectural conditions under which disciplined inquiry itself remains possible.

Those investigations eventually produced the first independent research papers published through SSRN.

Each paper answered a local question while exposing a deeper one.

Together they gradually revealed a coherent architectural direction.

---

## The Emergence of PortusSophia™

As the research matured, recurring structural themes became increasingly difficult to ignore.

Questions of distinction, translation, attribution, explanatory scope, and declared boundaries continued to appear across otherwise unrelated investigations.

Rather than forcing those observations into an existing discipline, the work gradually became architectural.

---

## Continuing the Work

PortusSophia™ remains an active independent research program.

Its purpose is not to establish authority or replace existing disciplines.

Its purpose is to investigate whether disciplined inquiry may itself possess architectural conditions worthy of independent study.

The inquiry continues.
